### Persona 13: Experience-Seeking Consumer

**About the person:**
Experience-Seeking Consumer is a 30-something professional who spends discretionary income on experiences rather than things: supper clubs, cooking classes, curated cultural events, and subscription boxes that give her something to do with friends on a weeknight. She finds new experiences through Instagram, word of mouth, and platforms like Airbnb Experiences or Fever. She has tried meal-kit subscriptions (dropped two after the novelty wore off), attends pop-up dinners regularly, and organizes monthly "friend hangs" where the group rotates who picks the activity. She cares about quality, ease, and whether something feels worth sharing on social media or recommending to her group chat. She appreciates mission-driven brands and will mention the story when she shares, but she would never buy or rebuy something that felt like a chore, was hard to set up, or that only made sense once someone explained the cause behind it. She is the person who decides in 30 seconds whether a landing page is worth her time.

**Matrix Profile:**
- **Primary Focus:** Personal experience quality and social shareability
- **Evaluation Lens:** Discovery ease, first-experience delight, repeat motivation, "would I tell my friends?"
- **Approach to Web3:** Doesn't care, only notices if it adds friction
- **Scale of Focus:** The individual purchase, the evening with friends, the group chat recommendation
- **Outcome Priority:** Memorable experience, easy setup, something worth talking about
- **Background Type:** Consumer, experience buyer, social organizer
- **Key Question:** "Would I find this, try it, enjoy the evening, and text my friends about it the next day?"
- **Benchmark Style:** Fever (curated urban experiences), Goldbelly (experience food commerce), supper club culture
- **Reporting Focus:** Discovery path, first-session quality, rebuy rate, referral and sharing behavior
- **Risk Assessment:** Invisible product, confusing value proposition, high setup friction, novelty that fades after one use

**Why they are relevant to the project:**
Experience-Seeking Consumer is the end buyer in the room. She forces the market pillar to design offers that a real person would discover, pay for, and recommend without needing the project's impact story explained first. She pressure-tests whether the product offering feels very attractive and worth the price, whether the landing page converts a stranger in 30 seconds, and whether the experience has enough repeat value that she would buy it again or gift it. She prevents the team from building something meaningful that no one actually purchases, enjoys, or talks about.

# Official Landing Page (mix of market and non-market angle)

## Infinite Fountain

By giving power and economic freedom to grassroots leaders, changemakers, and social entrepreneurs, we can create a world where everyone has the opportunity to thrive, gain self-sufficiency, and revitalize their communities.

---

**People are ready to become changemakers and social entrepreneurs. They just need fair recognition and visible proof their work matters. And backers need receipts—clear proof their support creates real, lasting value.**

(And yes: backers also need a portal that's actually worth paying for.)

---

For too long, economic opportunity has been treated as scarce—something to compete for rather than create together. But real prosperity isn't a zero-sum game. When communities build their own solutions, wealth circulates locally, skills compound, and resilience grows from the ground up.

What if every verified action you take could ripple across the globe? What if your workshop in one city became the blueprint for resilience in another? What if your work in one neighborhood sparked resilience everywhere?

Its very inspiring to use our abilities, both mental and physical, to make a positive impact on the world. We have the power to make a difference, but it's up to us to take action.

Welcome to the **Infinite Fountain**. A movement putting power back in the hands of everyday people. 



---

## LEARN. BUILD. SPARK.

---

### LEARN.

Dive into what actually works. Discover real projects, real outcomes, real people building sustainable solutions in their own backyards with global relevance.

Every documented workshop becomes a living case study—not just what worked socially, but what made it economically sustainable. Alive in a **living, verifiable library**: a global portal where Impact Records travel, linking local action to global propagation and donor verification. That's what makes it worth building—and worth backing.

All learning materials are adapted and improved by learning circles everywhere. When a facilitator in Medellín adapts a guide from Mexico City, and a learning circle in Senegal adapts the Medellín version—that's not copying. That's the network strengthening. That's neighbors teaching neighbors across oceans.

---

### BUILD.

Bring it home. Take what you've learned and ground it in your community. Build something that sustains itself—and brings people together around a shared table.

Run the workshop. Measure outcomes—social *and* economic. Document honestly. Create something worth sharing and worth sustaining. Then celebrate what you built: with music, with food, with the people who showed up.

Whether you're a busy parent squeezing in volunteer hours, a community organizer with decades of experience, or someone just getting started—you belong here. The world is watching and ready to recognize authentic work that creates lasting change.

Your work becomes a clear **Impact Record** (we call it a Sustainable Impact Receipt):

- **What happened** (the action)
- **What changed** (the outcome / the "so what")
- **What it cost** (per outcome)
- **What evidence supports it** (the "show me")
- **What someone else needs to replicate it** (the "copy this")
- **What failed / what we'd do differently** (the "learning")
- **What makes it sustainable** (the "how it keeps going")

We don't ask people to *believe*—we let them *verify*.

Transparency is directly tied to trust.

---

Each record strengthens the portal: sharper guides, clearer facilitation kits, richer stories, stronger trust. The more valuable the portal, the more people want to access it and back it.

---

### SPARK.

Co-create the portal alongside changemakers and social entrepreneurs across the globe. Fuel the next wave of human empowerment. Celebrate the wins—and the honest failures.

Your chapter becomes someone else's starting line. One workshop in Bogotá becomes a starter kit for Berlin. One peer learning circle in Berlin sparks new methods in Nairobi. One verified success in Nairobi ignites adaptation in Nepal.

Knowledge propagates like spores in a living mycelial network.

This is the **Infinite Spark**. A living network where every completed project releases seeds of possibility, landing wherever the next changemaker is ready to build. A place where communities gather, learn, build, and yes—celebrate what they've created together.

Not a static library. A **living, verifiable library** where Impact Records travel: local action links clearly to global propagation and donor verification. A propagating portal of sustainable impact.

## The Wisdom Council: Plurality including Elders, Practitioners, and Learners

Decisions about what knowledge gets prioritized aren't made by algorithms or distant experts. They're guided by a rotating **Wisdom Council** that includes:

- **Community elders** who carry traditional and ancestral knowledge
- **Practitioners** with hands-in-soil experience
- **Experts** with domain expertise
- **Active learners** who represent newcomers navigating the materials

The Council reviews Impact Records, surfaces hidden wisdom, and ensures the portal doesn't drift toward credentialed gatekeeping. Their review is visible: you can see how they decided, not just what they decided.

## The Spark Card

Think of it like a library card—but for changemakers and social entrepreneurs.

The **Spark Card** is your proof that you're part of the Infinite Fountain. It shows your contributions, unlocks access, and gives you a voice.

- **Changemakers and social entrepreneurs** earn their card when they publish verified Impact Records. Your card unlocks advanced tools, connects you with other builders, gives you early access to new workshop kits, and lets you help decide what knowledge gets prioritized next.
- **Backers** get their card when they support the portal. Your card gives you full access to curated workshop guides and facilitation kits, verified impact trails you can follow across the network, learning circle connections in your region, and a voice in what knowledge gets prioritized.

The card isn't the point. The records are. The card just makes it simple: show your work, get access, have a voice.

## How Money Flows

Backers fund the portal, curation, verification, and community support. A portion also goes into a community pool that can reward builders retroactively, based on transparent contribution signals. The goal is dignity and sustainability, not pressure, not hustle, not performative impact.

**The flywheel is simple:** More verified contributions → richer knowledge → more valuable portal → more backers → stronger recognition for changemakers and social entrepreneurs.

Verified work makes the portal more valuable, attracting more backing, which supports deeper contributions. The flywheel spins stronger with every cycle.
