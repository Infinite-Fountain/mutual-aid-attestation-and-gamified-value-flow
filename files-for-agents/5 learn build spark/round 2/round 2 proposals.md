## Idea 1: The Shared Table Procurement Coop

A pop-up-to-permanent cooperative kitchen that hires survivors and campesinas into paid shifts, sells to real buyers (not “charity”), and uses one-page Impact Receipts to make outcomes legible and trustworthy.

**First step (low complexity, high signal):**
Run **one** “Shared Table Night” pop-up:

* Produce **one** hero product line (e.g., tortillas + salsa, or preserves + tamales) with strict quality + on-time delivery standards.
* Sell **two** channels the same week:

  1. a ticketed community dinner (cash now)
  2. a small preorder batch for **two** reliable buyers (a café + an institution/office) (cash next week)
* Hand every attendee/buyer a **one-page Impact Receipt**: what you made, who got paid, what changed, what it cost, what didn’t work, what’s next.

**Long-term vision (economic flywheel + systemic change):**

* A **worker-owned commercial kitchen coop** with paid training shifts, clear graduation pathways, and **standing procurement contracts** (schools, hospitals, ethical corporate catering).
* A rotating “guest chef” model that turns graduates into visible professionals (social proof + career ladders).
* Public archive of receipts that makes impact harder to fake and easier to fund—while the core engine stays simple: **orders → paid shifts → stability → capacity → more orders**.
  Inspired by how Two Good describes scaling from small meals into a larger social enterprise with impact reported through stories + numbers. ([Two Good Co][1])

**Why it wins on your variables:**

* **Purpose + sustainable income:** paid shifts tied to real demand
* **People building something:** production, logistics, standards, delivery
* **Impact stories:** receipts become the habit + proof layer
* **Gathering + feeding:** community dinner is the stage, food is the bond
* **Mainstream appeal:** it’s an excellent dinner + great products first; impact is baked in
