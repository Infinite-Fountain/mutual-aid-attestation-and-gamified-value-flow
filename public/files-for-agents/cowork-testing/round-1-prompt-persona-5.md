## Business Model

What makes Two Good different from a charity is that its theory of change is also its business model.

The women aren't a cost centre. They are the business. It doesn't depend on the next grant cycle or the next gala dinner. It depends on customers. And the more customers it serves, the more women it can employ.

### A Model Born from Resilience

This model didn't start as a perfect blueprint. Two Good began as a buy-one-give-one catering service, but the team was losing money training unskilled staff and facing high turnover every few months. Rob asked: how do we turn this into value? The answer was to make the training itself the product. The Work Work program emerged not from a classroom, but from the necessity of a commercial kitchen.

### Revenue and Impact Scale Together

When a corporate team orders lunch or an office signs for washroom supplies, money enters as revenue first and becomes impact second. More orders mean more shifts. More shifts mean more wages, more confidence, more hours of real experience that translate into the next job.

In FY2025, 47 women were employed, generating 22,629 hours of paid work and over $726,000 in wages, all flowing directly from customer orders. Every major corporate catering contract creates approximately 120 hours of paid, trauma-informed work. Scaling does not dilute the mission. It intensifies it.

### Social Procurement Is the Sustainable Engine

The most durable revenue is not a spike; it is a contract. Two Good's strongest lever is B2B social procurement: large organisations shift spend they already have into a supplier that creates social value, turning an ESG commitment into predictable payroll.

Charter Hall's contribution in FY24 exceeded one million dollars, with over 70 percent from procurement of goods and services: close to 20,000 litres of washroom products a year, distributed through 5,000 touch-free dispensers made from ocean plastic, installed across 42 percent of its national office portfolio. The supplier sits inside the value chain, so impact is funded by normal operations, not leftover budgets.

Multi-year relationships reduce revenue volatility, which is what a trauma-informed employment program needs to be patient and consistent. Change happens at the speed of trust, and procurement turns trust into working capital.

### Real Demand, Not Goodwill

None of this works if the product is a pity purchase. The kitchen serves real clients who pay real money: corporate catering for the likes of Google and Atlassian, washroom and lifestyle products through ongoing procurement. A late delivery matters. A sloppy batch gets remade.

Excellence isn't an aesthetic; it's a strategy. Excellence is the opposite of patronage. Patronage whispers: we will buy this because we feel sorry for you. Excellence says: we buy this because it is genuinely good, and the impact is the bonus, not the excuse. Clinical evaluations by the Paul Ramsay Foundation confirmed this: the experience leads to clinically meaningful improvements in self-worth, confidence, and hope.

When a woman delivers a world-class product, she is not seeking pity; she is earning respect.

### From Bridge to Career

Through an Employment Partner Framework, graduates move into the workforces of corporate partners like Charter Hall, QBE, and GPT Group. Two Good provides the bridge; the partners provide the destination. In the latest year, 19 women were placed into ongoing employment. The final outcome is not just a meal. It is a career.
